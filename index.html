Henrietta Lacks

Woman who had the first line of Immortal Human Cells

Here is a timeline of Henrietta Lacks' Life

  • 1920 - Born in Roanoke, Virginia
  • 1935 - Gave birth to first son Lawrence Lacks
  • 1939 - Gave birth to first daughter Elsie Lacks
  • 1941 - Married to David Day in Halifax County
  • 1947 - Gave birth to David Lacks
  • 1949 - Gave birth to Deborah Lacks
  • 1950 - Gave birth to Joseph Lacks
  • 1951 - Went to Johns Hopkins for medical attention after feeling ill in August
    Also the time when two of her samples were given to George Otto Grey for research
  • 1951 - Dies at the age of 31 in Johns Hopkins Hospital in October

Medical and scientific research

  • George Otto Gey who first research Lack's cells discovered her cancerous cell reproduced in a high rate that allowed in-depth examination in compared to other cells who only survived a few days
  • Gey used the roller-tube method to culture and divide the cells for multiple uses, and it became know as HeLa Cell
  • Rapid production of HeLa cells allow for much medical breakthrough, including the first Polio Vaccine developed by Jonas Salk
  • Cells were mailed around the globe into research in field such as Cancer, AIDS, radiation and toxic substances, genes mapping
  • Almost around 11,000 Patents in HeLa Cells
